# Project Summary: LAI Clinical Trials Landscape

**Author:** Saeid Bazraei  

**GitHub:** [Samb-pharmed](https://github.com/Samb-pharmed)

## Objective

This project analyses the clinical-trial landscape of long-acting injectable products, depot formulations, implants, and extended-release injectable products using ClinicalTrials.gov API data.

The aim is to build a reproducible data science workflow that converts public clinical-trial records into structured benchmark outputs by product family, therapeutic area, phase, sponsor, enrolment, duration, and geography.

## Data Source

Clinical-trial data were retrieved from ClinicalTrials.gov API v2 using a curated dictionary of LAI, depot, implant, and extended-release injectable products.

The project includes the curated product dictionary in `data/reference/`. Raw and processed datasets are not included in the repository because they can be regenerated by running the scripts.

## Workflow

The workflow includes:

1. Creation of a curated LAI product dictionary.
2. API-based clinical-trial data extraction.
3. Deduplication by NCT ID.
4. Rule-based relevance scoring.
5. Creation of broad and strict analytical datasets.
6. Creation of a Phase 1–3 interventional development subset.
7. Exploratory data analysis.
8. Country-level development geography analysis.
9. Interactive Plotly map generation.
10. Product-family benchmarking.

## Analytical Datasets

Three main dataset levels were created:

| Dataset | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Broad dataset | Inclusive landscape exploration |
| Strict dataset | High-confidence LAI clinical-trial landscape |
| Development subset | Interventional Phase 1–3 trials for development benchmarking |

## Key Findings

The development subset included 1,792 interventional Phase 1–3 LAI-related clinical trials across 22 product families, 10 therapeutic areas, and 84 countries.

The major LAI development areas included addiction/substance use, GnRH/hormonal oncology, psychiatry/CNS, metabolic/diabetes, local anti-inflammatory, pain/local analgesia, and endocrine peptide depot therapy.

Median enrolment was more informative than mean enrolment because large registry or high-enrolment studies created outliers in the broader dataset.

The median enrolment in the development subset was 64 participants, and the median duration to primary completion was approximately 24 months.

## Geography Findings

Country-level analysis showed that the United States was the dominant geography in ClinicalTrials.gov-registered LAI Phase 1–3 development trials.

Important secondary development geographies included Canada, Germany, China, France, Spain, the United Kingdom, Italy, and Belgium.

An interactive development activity map was generated to visualise country-level LAI development intensity.

Selected figures are included in `reports/figures/`.

## Limitations

This project has several limitations:

- ClinicalTrials.gov is not a complete global registry.
- The dataset is influenced by US registration practices.
- Search-term based retrieval can produce false positives and false negatives.
- Relevance scoring and product-family classification are rule-based.
- Final commercial, regulatory, or clinical decisions would require manual validation.

## Future Work

Possible extensions include:

- Manual validation of the strict dataset.
- Inclusion of additional registries such as EU Clinical Trials Register, ISRCTN, and WHO ICTRP.
- NLP-based intervention role detection.
- Sponsor normalisation using a controlled sponsor dictionary.
- Streamlit or Dash dashboard development.
- Machine-learning models for enrolment or duration prediction.
